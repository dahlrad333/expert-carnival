STEMJobs

  • Summary

    A four-month long project to upgrade Ember website for STEM career portal.

  • Methods

    Brainstorming, front-end development, wireframing

  • Client

    End client Stem Jobs in conjunction with Victory Media [now defunct].

THE PROBLEM

Recently, there has been a large push toward developing more STEM workers. This has been viewed as a pipeline problem. Stem Jobs is addressing this with their website which is aimed at high school students. A core feature of this website is a quiz which helps students decide which career paths will be interesting to them.

THE PROCESS

My team and I worked with Victory Media for four months to understand the challenges Stem Jobs had faced in their goal. Once we had identified the pain points in usability, we were able to take action in implementing the solutions.

THE SOLUTION

One of our immediate observations was regarding the layout of the website was clumsy, and did not keep the attention of students. We spent the summer ideating and refining the overall design and iteratively developing a high fidelity prototype. In the end, the site had a more polished and modern look.

To support the new design, and address security concerns, we upgraded the Ember version being used.
