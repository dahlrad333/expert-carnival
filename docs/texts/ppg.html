OREO Colorfilled

  • Summary

    My team worked with the makers of the favorite cookie of the world to create a promotional web app to boost sales during the holidays.

  • Methods

    Front-end development, industrial printing, usability, wireframing

  • Client

    End client Mondelēz International makers of OREO Brand Cookies, in conjunction with The Martin Agency and MAYA Design [since acquired by Boston Consulting Group].

TRANSITION FROM COOKIE TO BRAND

Oreo was a well recognized a cookie, however, they wanted to push further to become one of the most iconic brands. To do so required big story telling. The answer lay in the human value of wonder, a value that everyone, regardless of age, can identify with. This renewed focus has contemporized the brand and sharpened their point-of-view. It has broadened thier appeal, outperforming all expectations, and positioned Oreo for the future.

CUSTOMIZATION TO THE RESCUE

There is a current trend of customization/personalization in consumer products. To cash in on this, Oreo created a interactive website where users could design and order their customized Oreo product.

THE PROCESS

We just helped Oreo launch Colorfilled—a direct-to-consumer platform where Oreo fans in the U.S. can digitally design unique holiday-themed packs of Oreos for friends and family. For the first time, Oreo customers will have custom packages delivered to their doorstep in 5-7 days thanks to this new e-commerce program. This is a key initiative within Mondelēz International’s overall e-commerce strategy, which aims at growing e-commerce revenues to $1 billion by 2020.

THE SOLUTION

We built a solution that levered technologies like AWS, Shopify, Ember, and uWSGI. We also had immense support from design and creative. It was truly a pleasure to be able to work on a team where everyone was an expert in their role.

With such a team, we were able to create the app from scratch in two months, ahead of our compressed timeline to deliver for the holiday season.
